Gaston Bronstering

PhD Candidate in International Relations at the London School of Economics

Gaston Bronstering is a PhD candidate in International Relations at the London School of Economics (LSE). Prior to LSE, he completed a BA and MA in International and European Governance at the University of Münster, the Diplôme d'Institut d'Études Politiques de Lille, and an MSc in Political Economy at the University of Amsterdam.

Gaston's research focuses on the economic and political influence of index providers and their market classifications. He is particularly interested in how these classifications impact global financial markets and the distribution of capital. His work combines quantitative analysis with qualitative case studies to explore the implications of index provider decisions on emerging markets and developing economies.
